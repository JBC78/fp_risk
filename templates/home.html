Authority To Work

During the month of July, a field verification audit on a work site was undertaken to validate the health of an existing Authority To Work process.

The audit was successful in completing 380 work front contacts over a 4 week period, covering 16 seperate companies with 19 different disiciplines, such as Industrial Cleaning (High Pressure Water, Ultra High Jetting, Industrial Vacuuming), Electrical, Mechanical, Rope Access and Condition Monitoring to name a few.

From the data collected, we were able to use use Machine Learning principals to predict the Risk Classification from the scope of work description provided - give it a go here!

Findings

(1) The data collated indicates that the Authority To Work process is unhealthy and operating at 57.70% compliance.
(2) The company representative is time poor and not able to visit all jobs in a timely manner, either delaying a job start or not visiting the job at all. Delayed job starts caused by the company impact the companys budget on the bottom line.
(3) Contracting Company GLU perform the most High risk work onsite across there three disciplines of Mechanical, Rope Acess & Scaffolding.
(4) Work performed between the hours of 0800 and 1400 have a 54% chance of not having a Job Front Review completed by the Company Representative. This allows potential unknown hazards or conditions to creep in to the work place unbeknown to the contract workers.

Findings Proposal

(1) Review the intent and framework of the Authority To Proceed procedure.
(2) Perform financial impact analysis to the business caused by delayed job starts due to the availability of the company representative.
